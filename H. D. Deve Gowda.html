H. D. Deve Gowda


H. D. Deve Gowda, whose full name is Haradanahalli Doddegowda Deve Gowda, is an Indian politician who served as the 11th Prime Minister of India. H. D. Deve Gowda's political career has spanned several decades, and he has been a significant player in the politics of Karnataka and the broader national political landscape. His term as Prime Minister was a crucial period marked by the complexities of coalition politics in India.

Early Life:

H. D. Deve Gowda was born on May 18, 1933, in Haradanahalli, Hassan district, Karnataka, India.

Political Entry:

Deve Gowda entered politics in the late 1950s and became associated with the Janata Party in the 1970s.

Chief Minister of Karnataka:

He served as the Chief Minister of Karnataka from 1994 to 1996. His tenure as Chief Minister was marked by efforts to address agricultural issues and rural development.

Prime Ministership:

H. D. Deve Gowda became the Prime Minister of India on June 12, 1996. His appointment as Prime Minister was a consensus decision among various non-BJP (Bharatiya Janata Party) and non-Congress parties that formed the United Front coalition.

Short Tenure and Coalition Politics:

Deve Gowda's tenure as Prime Minister was relatively short, lasting until April 21, 1997. His government faced challenges due to the dynamics of coalition politics and differences among supporting parties.

Economic Policies:

During his tenure, efforts were made to implement economic policies aimed at addressing issues such as poverty and unemployment.

Post-Prime Ministerial Career:

After his term as Prime Minister, Deve Gowda continued to be active in politics. He served as the Chief Minister of Karnataka for a brief period in 2004.

State and National Politics:

Deve Gowda has remained a prominent figure in Karnataka state politics and has been involved in national politics as well.
