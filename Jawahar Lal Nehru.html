Jawaharlal Nehru (1889–1964)


Jawaharlal Nehru (1889–1964) was a prominent political leader in India and a central figure in the country's struggle for independence from British rule. Jawaharlal Nehru's contributions to the Indian independence movement and the early years of independent India have left a lasting impact on the country's political, social, and economic development. His birthday, November 14th, is celebrated as Children's Day in India in his honor.

Early Life and Education:

  • Nehru was born on November 14, 1889, in Allahabad, British India, into a wealthy and politically influential family. He was educated in India and later studied at Harrow School in England and Trinity College, Cambridge.

Involvement in Independence Movement:

  • Nehru was deeply influenced by the ideals of Mahatma Gandhi and joined the Indian National Congress, the leading political party advocating for Indian independence. He played a crucial role in the non-violent civil disobedience campaigns organized by Gandhi, such as the Salt March in 1930.

Role in the Indian National Congress:

  • Nehru served as the President of the Indian National Congress several times. He was a key figure in the negotiations leading to Indian independence, representing India at the international level and participating in the
    framing of the Indian Constitution.

First Prime Minister of India:

  • Nehru became the first Prime Minister of independent India on August 15, 1947, when the country gained freedom from British rule. He held this position until his death in 1964, making him the longest-serving Prime Minister of India to date.

Policies and Vision:

  • Nehru's tenure as Prime Minister saw the implementation of several economic and social policies, including the initiation of five-year plans for economic development. He advocated for a secular and socialist India, emphasizing the importance of education and scientific temper.

Foreign Policy:

  • Nehru played a significant role in shaping India's foreign policy, promoting non-alignment during the Cold War and advocating for peaceful coexistence among nations.

Legacy:

  • Nehru is often referred to as the architect of modern India due to his role in laying the foundations of democratic governance, economic planning, and secularism. His daughter, Indira Gandhi, and grandson, Rajiv Gandhi, also served as Prime Ministers of India

Death:

  • Jawaharlal Nehru passed away on May 27, 1964.
