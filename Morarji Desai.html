Morarji Desai (1896–1995)


Morarji Desai (1896–1995) was an Indian independence activist and politician who served as the Prime Minister of India. Morarji Desai was known for his commitment to Gandhian principles, austerity, and simplicity. His tenure as Prime Minister marked a significant phase in India's political history, especially as the head of the first non-Congress government at the center.

Early Life and Education:

  • Morarji Desai was born on February 29, 1896, in Bhadeli, Bombay Presidency (now in Gujarat, India). He studied at Wilson College in Mumbai and later attended the Government Law College.

Involvement in the Independence Movement:

  • Desai actively participated in the Indian independence movement and joined Mahatma Gandhi's Non-Cooperation Movement in 1930. He became a prominent leader in the Indian National Congress and the Gujarat Sabha.

Prison Term during Quit India Movement:

  • During the Quit India Movement in 1942, Desai was arrested by British authorities and spent several years in prison

Post-Independence Political Career:

  • After India gained independence in 1947, Morarji Desai held several key positions in the government of independent India. He served as the Chief Minister of the Bombay State (1952–1956) and later as the Chief Minister of the reorganized state of Bombay (1956–1957).

Finance Minister and Economic Reforms:

  • Desai served as the Finance Minister of India from 1959 to 1963 under Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru. He introduced significant economic reforms, including the devaluation of the rupee in 1966.

Split from the Congress Party:

  • Morarji Desai had ideological differences with Prime Minister Indira Gandhi, leading to his resignation from the Congress Party in 1969.

Janata Party and Emergency:

  • He became a key figure in the formation of the Janata Party, a coalition of opposition parties. Morarji Desai was instrumental in uniting the diverse opposition forces against the Congress Party during the 1977 elections.

Prime Ministership (1977–1979):

  • Morarji Desai became the Prime Minister of India in 1977, leading the Janata Party to victory in the post-Emergency elections. His government focused on promoting austerity, anti-corruption measures, and the improvement of relations with neighboring countries.

Resignation and Later Years:

  • Internal conflicts within the Janata Party led to Desai's resignation in 1979. After his political career, Morarji Desai continued to be involved in public life and wrote books on holistic health practices.

Death:

  • Morarji Desai passed away on April 10, 1995, at the age of 99.
