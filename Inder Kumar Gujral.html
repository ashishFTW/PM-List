Inder Kumar Gujral (1919–2012)


Inder Kumar Gujral (1919–2012) was an Indian politician who served as the 12th Prime Minister of India. Inder Kumar Gujral is remembered for his contributions to Indian diplomacy and his efforts to promote regional cooperation and peaceful relations with neighboring countries. The Gujral Doctrine, in particular, reflects his vision for a stable and cooperative South Asia.

Early Life:

Inder Kumar Gujral was born on December 4, 1919, in Jhelum, British India (now in Pakistan). He was educated at Forman Christian College in Lahore.

Partition and Refugee Camps:

After the partition of India in 1947, Gujral and his family migrated to India and settled in Delhi. He worked in refugee camps and was deeply affected by the human suffering he witnessed during that time.

Diplomatic Career:

Gujral joined the Indian Foreign Service in 1949 and had a distinguished diplomatic career. He served as India's ambassador to the Soviet Union, Nepal, and Belgium.

Political Entry:

Gujral entered Indian politics and joined the Congress Party in the 1960s. He held various ministerial positions, including Minister of Information and Broadcasting.

Janata Dal and Coalition Politics:

Gujral later joined the Janata Dal party and became a prominent leader in the context of coalition politics. He served as the Minister of External Affairs under Prime Minister V. P. Singh.

Prime Ministership:

Inder Kumar Gujral became the Prime Minister of India on April 21, 1997. His tenure as Prime Minister was marked by a focus on improving relations with India's neighbors.

Gujral Doctrine:

The Gujral Doctrine, articulated by him during his tenure as Foreign Minister, emphasized goodwill and non-reciprocal gestures towards India's neighboring countries. The doctrine aimed at building trust and goodwill in the region.

Death:

Inder Kumar Gujral passed away on November 30, 2012, in Gurgaon, Haryana, India.
