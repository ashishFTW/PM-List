Manmohan Singh


Manmohan Singh is an Indian economist, academic, and politician who served as the 13th Prime Minister of India from 2004 to 2014. Manmohan Singh is often recognized for his role in shaping India's economic policies and for his contributions to the country's development. His tenure as Prime Minister is marked by both achievements and challenges, and he is widely respected for his integrity and economic expertise.

Early Life and Education:

  • Manmohan Singh was born on September 26, 1932, in Gah, Punjab (now in Pakistan). He obtained his bachelor's and master's degrees in economics from Panjab University, Chandigarh.

Academic Career:

  • Singh pursued an academic career and earned a D.Phil. in economics from the University of Oxford. He worked as a professor of international trade at the Delhi School of Economics and later served as the Chief Economic Adviser in the Ministry of Finance.

Economic Reforms (1991):

  • Manmohan Singh played a crucial role in formulating and implementing economic reforms in India in 1991 when he served as the Finance Minister under Prime Minister P. V. Narasimha Rao. The reforms aimed at liberalizing the Indian economy and dismantling the License Raj.

Governor of Reserve Bank of India:

  • Singh served as the Governor of the Reserve Bank of India from 1982 to 1985.

Political Career:

  • Manmohan Singh entered active politics and became a member of the Rajya Sabha in 1991. He held various ministerial positions, including Finance Minister, Minister of External Affairs, and Leader of the Opposition in the Rajya Sabha.

Prime Ministership:

  • Manmohan Singh became the Prime Minister of India in 2004 after the Indian National Congress-led coalition, the United Progressive Alliance (UPA), won the general elections. He was re-elected for a second term in 2009.

Resignation:

  • Manmohan Singh did not seek a third term as Prime Minister, and he resigned from office in May 2014 after the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) won the general elections.

Later Years:

  • After leaving office, Singh continued to be active in public life. He has been a vocal advocate for economic reforms, inclusive growth, and social justice.
