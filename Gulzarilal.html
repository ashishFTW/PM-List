Gulzarilal Nanda (1898–1998)


Gulzarilal Nanda (1898–1998) was an Indian politician who played a significant role in the early years of independent India. Gulzarilal Nanda is remembered for his service to the nation during a critical period in India's history, especially for his role as the Acting Prime Minister during times of transition. His contributions to labor and economic policies have left a lasting impact on India's development.

Early Life and Education:

Gulzarilal Nanda was born on July 4, 1898, in Sialkot, British India (now in Pakistan). He studied at Lahore's Forman Christian College and later pursued a degree in economics from the University of Allahabad.

Political Career:

Nanda was actively involved in the Indian independence movement and joined the Indian National Congress. He worked closely with leaders like Jawaharlal Nehru and was associated with the trade union movement.

Contribution to Independent India:

Gulzarilal Nanda played a crucial role in the economic and labor policies of independent India. He served as the Labour Minister in the Interim Government in 1946-47 and later became the Planning Minister.

Acting Prime Minister:

Nanda served as the Acting Prime Minister of India twice: The first time was after the death of Jawaharlal Nehru in 1964. Nanda served as the Acting Prime Minister until Lal Bahadur Shastri took over. The second time was after the sudden demise of Lal Bahadur Shastri in 1966. Nanda again served as the Acting Prime Minister until the election of Indira Gandhi.

Cabinet Positions:

In addition to serving as the Acting Prime Minister, Nanda held several important cabinet positions during his political career, including Home Minister and Finance Minister.

Post-Political Life:

After retiring from active politics, Gulzarilal Nanda remained involved in social and educational activities. He continued to contribute to public life and was associated with various organizations.

Awards and Recognition:

Nanda received the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 1997, a year before his death, in recognition of his significant contributions to the nation.

Death:

Gulzarilal Nanda passed away on January 15, 1998, at the age of 99.
